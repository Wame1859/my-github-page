Concept

The Female Gaze Archive examines how visual construction produces authority. It studies framing, posture, costume and silence as deliberate aesthetic decisions rather than decorative elements.

Rather than focusing on narrative plot, this archive isolates visual moments where composition directs perception. Each entry considers how structure, discipline and spatial arrangement shape the viewer's understanding of power.

The project treats image as architecture rather than ornament, positioning femininity as strategic rather than passive.
